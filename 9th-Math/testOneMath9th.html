Class 9 CBSE – Paper 1

October 23, 2025 (Thursday)

Section A – 1 mark each

1. Write the decimal expansion of 1/7.
\( \frac{1}{7} = 0.\overline{142857} \)
Non-terminating recurring decimal. Rational number.
2. Find the degree of the polynomial \( 3x^4 - 5x^2 + 7 \).
Degree = 4
3. What is the x-coordinate where the graph intersects the x-axis?
At x-axis, y = 0. Solve the equation for x.
4. State Euclid’s first axiom.
Things which are equal to the same thing are equal to one another.
5. If two lines intersect, how many common points do they have?
One point.

Section B – 2 marks each

6. Rationalize \( \frac{1}{\sqrt{3} + \sqrt{2}} \).
Multiply by conjugate:
\( \frac{1}{\sqrt{3} + \sqrt{2}} \cdot \frac{\sqrt{3} - \sqrt{2}}{\sqrt{3} - \sqrt{2}} = \frac{\sqrt{3} - \sqrt{2}}{1} \)
7. Factorize \( x^2 - 5x + 6 \).
\( x^2 - 5x + 6 = (x - 2)(x - 3) \)
8. Plot the point (3, –2) and mention the quadrant.
Quadrant IV (x > 0, y < 0)
9. Write the equation: “Sum of a number and 5 is 9.”
Let number be x: \( x + 5 = 9 \)
10. If ∠A + ∠B = 180°, what is the relation between AB and CD?
AB ∥ CD (co-interior angles)

Section C – 3 marks each

11. Find a if \( x^2 + ax + 6 \) has (–2) as a zero.
Substitute x = –2: \( 4 - 2a + 6 = 0 \Rightarrow a = 5 \)
12. Show that \( \sqrt{5} \) is irrational.
Assume \( \sqrt{5} = \frac{p}{q} \Rightarrow p^2 = 5q^2 \). Contradiction ⇒ irrational.
13. Draw the graph of \( x + y = 4 \).
Points: (0,4), (2,2), (4,0). Draw a straight line through these.
14. Prove vertically opposite angles are equal.
Use intersecting lines diagram: ∠A = ∠D, ∠B = ∠C
15. In triangle ABC, AB = AC and ∠B = 50°, find ∠C and ∠A.
∠C = 50°, ∠A = 80°

Section D – 4 marks each

16. Divide \( 2x^3 + 3x^2 - 5x + 6 \) by \( x - 2 \).
Quotient: \( 2x^2 + 7x + 9 \), Remainder: 24
17. Prove sum of angles in triangle is 180°.
Use angle sum property: ∠A + ∠B + ∠C = 180°
18. In triangle XYZ, XY = XZ and ∠Y = 40°. Find all angles.
∠Z = 40°, ∠X = 100°
19. Using Euclid’s axioms, prove: equals added to equals are equal.
Let a = b, c = d ⇒ a + c = b + d
20. Prove: In triangle, sides opposite equal angles are equal.
If ∠A = ∠B ⇒ side opposite ∠A = side opposite ∠B
