Chapter 1: Number System

Write the decimal expansion of 1/7. Is it terminating or non-terminating?
1/7 = 0.142857... (repeating). It is non-terminating and recurring, hence rational.
Represent √5 on the number line geometrically.
Construct a right triangle with legs 2 and 1 units. Hypotenuse = √5. Use compass to mark it on the number line.
Rationalize the denominator of 1/(√7 + √6).
Multiply by conjugate: \( \frac{1}{\sqrt{7} + \sqrt{6}} \times \frac{\sqrt{7} - \sqrt{6}}{\sqrt{7} - \sqrt{6}} = \sqrt{7} - \sqrt{6} \)
Find two rational numbers between √2 and √3.
√2 ≈ 1.41, √3 ≈ 1.73. Rational numbers between them: 1.5 and 1.6.
Express 0.333... as a rational number.
Let x = 0.333... ⇒ 10x = 3.333... ⇒ 9x = 3 ⇒ x = 1/3
Is π a rational or irrational number? Justify your answer.
π is irrational. Its decimal expansion is non-terminating and non-repeating: 3.14159...

Chapter 2: Polynomials

Find the degree of the polynomial 5x³ - 2x² + x - 7.
The degree is 3 (highest power of x).
If p(x) = x² - 3x + 2, find p(1), p(2), and p(0).
p(1) = 0, p(2) = 0, p(0) = 2
Factorize x² - 5x + 6.
x² - 5x + 6 = (x - 2)(x - 3)
Show that x = 2 is a zero of the polynomial x² - 4x + 4.
Substitute x = 2: 4 - 8 + 4 = 0 ⇒ x = 2 is a zero.
Divide x³ + 3x² + 3x + 1 by x + 1.
Using long division or identity: (x + 1)³ = x³ + 3x² + 3x + 1

Chapter 3: Linear Equations in Two Variables

Write the standard form of a linear equation in two variables.
Ax + By + C = 0, where A, B, and C are real numbers and A, B ≠ 0.
Find two solutions of the equation 2x + 3y = 6.
x = 0 ⇒ y = 2; x = 3 ⇒ y = 0
Draw the graph of the equation x + y = 4.
Plot points like (0,4), (2,2), (4,0) and draw a straight line through them.
If x = 1, find the value of y in the equation 3x + 2y = 7.
3(1) + 2y = 7 ⇒ 2y = 4 ⇒ y = 2
Write a linear equation whose graph passes through the points (0, 2) and (2, 0).
Slope = (0 - 2)/(2 - 0) = -1 ⇒ Equation: x + y = 2

Chapter 4: Linear Inequalities

Solve: 3x - 5 < 7
3x < 12 ⇒ x < 4
Graph the inequality x ≥ 2 on the number line.
Draw a solid dot at x = 2 and shade to the right.
Is x = -1 a solution of the inequality 2x + 3 > 0?
2(-1) + 3 = 1 > 0 ⇒ Yes, it is a solution.

Chapter 5: Lines and Angles

State the vertically opposite angles theorem.
When two lines intersect, the opposite angles are equal.
If two angles are supplementary and one is 65°, find the other.
180° - 65° = 115°
Define a linear pair of angles.
Two adjacent angles whose non-common arms form a straight line (sum = 180°).

Chapter 6: Triangles

State the Pythagoras theorem.
In a right triangle: (Hypotenuse)² = (Base)² + (Height)²
Prove that angles opposite equal sides of a triangle are equal.
Use congruence: If AB = AC, then triangle ABC is isosceles ⇒ ∠B = ∠C
What is the sum of interior angles of a triangle?
180°

Chapter 7: Quadrilaterals

Define a parallelogram.
A quadrilateral with opposite sides parallel and equal.
State the angle sum property of a quadrilateral.
Sum of interior angles = 360°
If one angle of a parallelogram is 70°, find its adjacent angle.
Adjacent angles are supplementary ⇒ 180° - 70° = 110°

Chapter 8: Circles

Define a chord of a circle.
A line segment joining two points on the circle.
State the angle subtended by a diameter at the circle.
It subtends a right angle (90°) at the circumference.
What is the relation between radius and diameter?
Diameter = 2 × Radius

Chapter 9: Statistics

Define mean of a data set.
Mean = (Sum of observations) ÷ (Number of observations)
Find the mode of: 2, 3, 4, 4, 5, 4, 6
Mode = 4 (occurs most frequently)
What is the median of: 5, 7, 9, 11, 13?
Median = 9 (middle value)

Chapter 10: Probability

Define probability.
Probability = (Favorable outcomes) ÷ (Total outcomes)
What is the probability of getting a head when tossing a coin?
1/2
Find the probability of getting an even number when rolling a die.
Even numbers: 2, 4, 6 ⇒ Probability = 3/6 = 1/2
